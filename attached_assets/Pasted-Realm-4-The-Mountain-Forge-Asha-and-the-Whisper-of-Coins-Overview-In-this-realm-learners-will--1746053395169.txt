ü™® 4.1 The Power of the Puzzle ‚Äî How Bitcoin Mining Works
Content:
Bitcoin mining is the process by which new bitcoins are created and transactions are confirmed on the blockchain. It relies on hashing, which is a way to convert data into a fixed-size string of characters. Miners use powerful computers to solve complex mathematical puzzles, creating blocks that hold transaction data.
Hashing: Each block contains a unique hash that links it to the previous block, forming a chain. This ensures that once a block is added, it cannot be altered without changing all subsequent blocks.
Block Creation: When miners solve a puzzle, they create a new block and receive a reward in bitcoins, which incentivizes them to continue mining.
Difficulty Adjustment: Every 2016 blocks, the Bitcoin network adjusts the difficulty of puzzles to ensure that a block is mined approximately every 10 minutes, regardless of how many miners are active.
Challenge Game:
Block Puzzle Challenge: Arrange scrambled pieces of a block to form a complete block. Each correct arrangement earns points and teaches about the components of a block.

üõ°Ô∏è 4.2 The Role of Miners ‚Äî Security Through Consensus
Content:
Miners play a crucial role in maintaining the security and integrity of the Bitcoin network. They validate transactions and ensure consensus through the longest chain rule, which means that the chain with the most computational work is considered the valid one.
Preventing Double Spending: By confirming transactions across the network, miners prevent the same bitcoin from being spent twice.
Types of Nodes: Full nodes maintain a complete copy of the blockchain, miners confirm transactions, and non-mining nodes relay information.
